The Most Mid PUBG Squad Known To Men

We're talking about a squad that's not just average but slightly below that, with a knack for constant trolling, and a tendency to mess around more than they focus on winning. This squad might be the last to land, the last to loot, and often the first to get wiped out. They’re the kind of team that might accidentally blow up their own vehicle, miscommunicate and experience low-fps during crucial moments, and end up losing most gunfights in hilariously unfortunate ways if they are lucky, unless their PC shuts off immediately due to high temperatures. Despite their lack of talent, zombie-ass computers and tendency to troll, they still have fun and embrace the chaos, turning every match into a comedic experience rather than a competitive one.

Most Common Mishaps

Low-FPS

Their constant low FPS is like playing PUBG in a flipbook, where every movement is jittery and delayed. A simple task like aiming down sights turns into an exercise in patience, as the crosshair drags across the screen like it’s wading through molasses. They might see an enemy, but by the time they react, the enemy has moved, and they’re left shooting at empty air. It’s a bit like trying to win a race while running through a swamp—slow, frustrating, and often ending in disaster.

Stuttering

The stuttering is a special kind of torment, where the game freezes for just long enough to miss the crucial shot, then fast-forwards to the inevitable “You Died” screen. It’s as if the game itself is trolling them, picking the worst moments to freeze, like when they’re about to engage in a firefight or when they’re driving full speed into a compound. The world around them glitches out, and by the time it settles, they’ve usually crashed into a tree or taken a bullet to the head.

PC Thermal Shutdown

The ultimate betrayal comes when their PC decides it’s had enough and just shuts down, mid-game. One moment they’re in the thick of it, strategizing and coordinating with the team, and the next, they’re staring at a blue screen as their PC overheats. The rest of the squad is left wondering where their teammate went, only to realize they’ve been booted from the game by their own hardware. It’s a harsh reminder that in the battle between PUBG and thermodynamics, sometimes thermodynamics wins.
